# AK-17 · Helicity as topological invariant

## Approach tried

Use helicity $H$ as a conserved topological invariant measuring
vortex-line linking.

## Obstruction

(a) NS helicity not conserved: $dH/ds = -2\nu \int \omega \cdot
\mathrm{curl}(\omega) \neq 0$ generally.  (b) Globally undefined
for $v \in L^{3,\infty}$ (integral of $1/|x|^3$ diverges).
(c) Viscous reconnection destroys linking numbers.  Topological
invariants die under NS; live only for Euler (inviscid).
